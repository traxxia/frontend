# Strategic Analysis Overview

Strategic Analysis in Traxxia transforms your questionnaire answers into actionable strategic frameworks. Using AI-powered analysis, Traxxia generates comprehensive visualizations and insights to guide your business strategy.

---

## What is Strategic Analysis?

Strategic Analysis takes the information you provided in the AI questionnaire and generates structured frameworks that help you:

- Understand your competitive position
- Identify strategic opportunities and threats
- Evaluate market forces
- Assess internal capabilities
- Make data-driven strategic decisions

---

## Available Strategic Frameworks

Traxxia provides multiple analytical frameworks, each with a specific strategic purpose:

### 1. SWOT Analysis
**Purpose**: Identify internal Strengths & Weaknesses, external Opportunities & Threats

**What It Shows**:
- Internal strengths to leverage
- Weaknesses to address
- Market opportunities to pursue
- External threats to mitigate

**Best For**: Overall strategic assessment and planning

![SWOT Analysis framework example](/academy-screenshots/strategic-analysis/full-swot.png)

---

### 2. PESTEL Analysis
**Purpose**: Evaluate macro-environmental factors affecting your business

**Components**:
- **Political**: Government policies, regulations, trade restrictions
- **Economic**: Economic growth, interest rates, inflation
- **Social**: Demographics, cultural trends, consumer attitudes
- **Technological**: Innovation, automation, tech adoption
- **Environmental**: Sustainability, climate impact, resource scarcity
- **Legal**: Laws, compliance requirements, industry regulations

**Best For**: Understanding external forces and long-term planning

![PESTEL Analysis framework example](/academy-screenshots/strategic-analysis/pestel.png)

---

### 3. Porter's Five Forces
**Purpose**: Analyze competitive dynamics and industry attractiveness

**Five Forces**:
1. **Competitive Rivalry**: Intensity of competition
2. **Supplier Power**: Bargaining power of suppliers
3. **Buyer Power**: Bargaining power of customers
4. **Threat of Substitutes**: Alternative solutions
5. **Threat of New Entrants**: Barriers to entry

**Best For**: Competitive strategy and market entry decisions

![Porter's Five Forces analysis](/academy-screenshots/strategic-analysis/porter.png)

---

### 4. Strategic Positioning Radar
**Purpose**: Visual representation of competitive positioning across key dimensions

**Dimensions**:
- Market share
- Innovation capability
- Customer satisfaction
- Operational efficiency
- Financial performance
- Brand strength

**Best For**: Quick visual comparison with competitors

![Strategic Positioning Radar chart](/academy-screenshots/strategic-analysis/strategic-position.png)

---

### 5. Competitive Advantage Analysis
**Purpose**: Identify and articulate your unique competitive advantages

**What It Shows**:
- Core differentiators
- Sources of competitive advantage
- Sustainability of advantages
- Areas for improvement

**Best For**: Differentiation strategy and market positioning

---

### 6. Purchase Criteria Analysis
**Purpose**: Understand what drives customer buying decisions

**Analysis Includes**:
- Key decision factors
- Factor importance weighting
- Your performance vs. competitors
- Strategic implications

**Best For**: Product development and sales strategy

---

### 7. Competitive Landscape
**Purpose**: Map and analyze your competitive environment

**What It Shows**:
- Direct and indirect competitors
- Competitive positioning
- Market segments
- Strategic groups

**Best For**: Competitive intelligence and market strategy

---

## How Strategic Analysis Works

### Step 1: Complete AI Questionnaire
Answer questions across all phases (especially Essential phase)

### Step 2: AI Processing
Traxxia's AI analyzes your answers and generates frameworks

### Step 3: Framework Generation
Click "Generate" buttons to create specific analyses

### Step 4: Review & Iterate
Review insights, refine answers if needed, regenerate

---

## Generating Your First Analysis

### Access Strategic Analysis:
1. Open your business
2. Click **"Insight 6 C's"** tab
3. Scroll through available frameworks

### Generate a Framework:
1. Find the framework card (e.g., "SWOT Analysis")
2. Click the "Generate" button
3. Wait for AI processing (15-60 seconds)
4. Review generated insights

![Generate button on SWOT framework card](/academy-screenshots/strategic-analysis/full-swot.png)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> You must complete the Essential phase of the questionnaire before generating most strategic analyses. Incomplete answers result in limited or generic insights.

## Regenerating Analyses

### When to Regenerate:
- Updated questionnaire answers significantly
- Business circumstances changed
- Want to refine previous analysis
- Need fresh perspective

### How to Regenerate:
1. Navigate to the framework
2. Click "Regenerate" button (circular arrow icon)
3. Confirm regeneration
4. Wait for new analysis

> [!WARNING]
> Regenerating overwrites the previous analysis. If you want to preserve it, export or screenshot first.

## Best Practices

### 1. Complete Essential Questions First
Generate analyses AFTER thoroughly answering Essential phase questions for best results.

### 2. Start with SWOT
SWOT provides a comprehensive overview - start here before diving into specific frameworks.

### 3. Cross-Reference Frameworks
Use multiple frameworks together:
- SWOT highlights what matters
- PESTEL explains external context
- Porter's shows competitive forces

### 4. Review with Your Team
Strategic analyses spark discussion - review with stakeholders and team members.

### 5. Update Regularly
Regenerate quarterly or when significant business changes occur.

## Common Questions

**Q: How does the AI generate these analyses?**  
A: Traxxia's AI uses natural language processing to extract strategic insights from your questionnaire answers, then structures them into established strategic frameworks.

**Q: Can I edit the generated analyses?**  
A: Not directly in the current version. To change the analysis, update your questionnaire answers and regenerate.

**Q: How accurate are the analyses?**  
A: Accuracy depends entirely on the quality and completeness of your questionnaire answers. Honest, detailed answers produce accurate, actionable insights.

**Q: Do I need all frameworks?**  
A: No. Generate only the frameworks relevant to your strategic questions. SWOT and PESTEL are most universally useful.

**Q: Can I share analyses with others?**  
A: Yes, via PDF export or by inviting collaborators to your business who can view the analyses directly.
