
## Moving from Strategy to Execution

The transition from a strategic vision to on-the-ground execution is a core part of the Traxxia experience. This guide explains how your strategic priorities are identified, where to manage them, and how to kickstart them into live projects.

## 1. The Executive Summary: Your Strategic Roadmap
Immediately after completing the **PMF Onboarding** flow, you are presented with the **Executive Summary**. This page serves as your high-level strategic roadmap, distilling your data into three critical sections:

- **WHERE TO COMPETE**: Defines your current core and suggested adjacencies.
- **HOW TO COMPETE**: Outlines your differentiation strategy.
- **TOP 3-5 PRIORITIES**: Identifies the most impactful workstreams for your business.

## 2. The Priorities Tab
While the Executive Summary provides the overview, the **Priorities** tab is where you take action. Here, your strategic priorities are listed as interactive cards.

- **Priority Cards**: Each card represents a high-level strategic goal.
- **Tactical Projects**: Expanding a card reveals the specific, tactical actions or projects bundled within that priority.
- **State Awareness**: The system tracks which priorities have already been converted into projects.

## 3. The Kickstart Flow
The Kickstart process is designed for **users with execution access** and **Admin** privileges (Company Admin or Super Admin).

### **Step 1: Selection**
Use the checkboxes on the left of each priority card to select the initiatives you are ready to execute. You can select a single priority or multiple items for bulk kickstarting.

![Priority Selection](/academy-screenshots/projects/kickstart-overview-banner.png)
*Screenshot: Selecting priorities in the Priorities tab*

### **Step 2: Execution**
Click the **Kickstart Projects** rocket button. Traxxia will then:
1. Clone each tactical action into a **Draft Project**.
2. Populate project fields (description, metrics, etc.) using AI-derived strategic data.
3. Move these projects to your **Projects** tab for further refinement.

### **Step 3: Post-Kickstart Status**
Once a priority is kickstarted:
- The card is marked with a **"Kickstarted"** badge and a green checkmark.
- The checkbox for that priority is disabled, preventing duplicate project creation.
- You can still browse other priorities and kickstart them at any time to add to your existing project list.

![Kickstarted Status](/academy-screenshots/projects/kickstart-post-kickstart-banner.png)
*Screenshot: A priority card showing the "Kickstarted" status*

## 4. Access & Plan Restrictions
- **Plans without Kickstart Access**: You can view all priorities and projects but cannot kickstart them. Clicking the button will prompt an **Upgrade Required** modal.
- **Collaborators**: Can view the priorities but do not have the selection or kickstart buttons visible.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Kickstarting is a one-way transition. Once an initiative becomes a project, it moves into a collaborative execution lifecycle where you can assign owners, set budgets, and track progress.
